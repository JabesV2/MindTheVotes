How to avoid election misinformation and propaganda?

Fact-check sites and verify information

Certain websites such as PolitiFact and Snopes can be used to verify and debunk false information, images, statements, and videos as well.

Look for multiple sources

Searching for multiple sources plays an important role especially in terms of wanting to figure out whether the information you've received is false or not. Do not just solely rely on just one source, but instead, on multiple sources. Especially those with a reputation for legit accuracy.

Be skeptical

If something seems a bit too good to be true, then you may consider fact-checking first because it most likely is designed to mislead.

Characteristics of a Responsible Voter

A responsible voter demonstrates several key characteristics that ensure their participation in the democratic process is informed, thoughtful, and effective. Here are some important traits:

  • Informed: A responsible voter takes the time to educate themselves about candidates, policies, and political issues. They seek information from credible and diverse sources to form a well-rounded understanding of the election and its implications.
  • Critical Thinker: Rather than relying on hearsay or partisan rhetoric, responsible voters critically analyze information, consider multiple viewpoints, and engage with facts. They question assumptions and make decisions based on logic and evidence.
  • Non-partisan: While many voters have party affiliations, a responsible voter is open to voting for candidates based on their qualifications, ideas, and values, not just party lines. They prioritize policies and candidates that best align with their values and the common good, rather than simply following political loyalty.
  • Active Participation: Responsible voters don't just vote in elections; they stay engaged in the political process year-round. They participate in community discussions, stay informed about ongoing issues, and may engage in advocacy or activism to promote policies they believe in.
  • Open-Minded: A responsible voter is open to changing their mind when presented with new, compelling information. They listen to diverse perspectives and understand that their views may evolve over time.

Steps to Ensure Informed Voting

Research the Candidates

  • Review candidates' backgrounds, platforms, and past leadership experience.
  • Check their stance on key issues like economy, education, health, and national security.
  • Be aware of political dynasties and track records of corruption.

Understand the Positions Being Elected

  • Research what each government position entails (e.g., President, Senator, Governor, Mayor, Barangay Officials).
  • Know their responsibilities to assess candidates properly.

Fact-Check Claims and Avoid Misinformation

  • Use fact-checking platforms like VERA Files, Rappler's Fact-Check, and ABS-CBN's Tsek.ph.
  • Be critical of viral posts, memes, and chain messages that spread false information.

Use Reliable and Independent Sources

  • Follow credible news organizations like Rappler, Philippine Daily Inquirer, CNN Philippines, or government sources.
  • Check COMELEC's official website for legitimate candidate information.
  • Avoid biased blogs, fake news sites, and misleading social media posts.

Significance of Voting in a Democracy

  • Expression of People's Will – Voting allows citizens to express their opinions on how they want their country to be governed.
  • Accountability of Leaders – Through elections, citizens can hold their leaders accountable by voting them out if they fail to deliver on their promises.
  • Promotion of Equality – Every vote carries the same weight, ensuring that all citizens, regardless of their background, have an equal say in decision-making.
  • Protection of Rights and Freedoms – By participating in elections, voters help select leaders who will uphold democratic principles and protect individual rights.
  • Encourages Civic Engagement – Voting promotes political awareness and participation, leading to a more informed society.
